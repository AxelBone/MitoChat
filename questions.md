#### NBK1490 - Pantothenate Kinase-Associated Neurodegeneration

Question 1 : Quels sont les médicaments de première intention les plus souvent efficaces pour traiter la dystonie chez les patients atteints de PKAN ?

- Oral baclofen, trihexyphenidyl, and clonazepam: the first-line drugs most commonly effective in PKAN (section "Treatment of Manifestations", page 11)

Question 2 : Quel est le mode de transmission (MOI) de la neuroferritinopathie ? 

- AD (Tableau 2, ligne "FTL" Neuorferritinopathy), colonne "MOI", page 8.

Question 3 : Quelle est la cause la plus probabl de décès prématuré dans les cas de PKAN ? 

- Premature death does occur. However, with improvements in medical care, a greater number of affected individuals are living into adulthood. Orofacial dystonia can result in the secondary effects of swallowing difficulty and poor nutrition. Premature death is more likely related to these secondary effects (e.g., nutrition-related immunodeficiency, aspiration pneumonia) than to the primary neurodegenerative process. In rare cases death occurs during status dystonicus.(section "Prognosis", page 6)

Question 4 : Quel pourcentage de variants pathogènes est identifié par l'analyse séquentielle du gène PANK2 chez les individus présentant le signe de "l'oeil du tigre" à l'IRM ? 

- \>99% of individuals with NBIA with "eye of the tiger" sign on MRI 4, 5 ~50% of individuals with clinical diagnosis of NBIA (Table 1, lign "Sequence analysis", page 5)

Question 5 : Les porteurs hétérozygotes (asymptomatiques) d'un variant pathogène du gène PANK2 sont-ils à risque de développer la maladie ?

- The parents of an affected child are expected to be obligate heterozygotes (i.e., carriers of one PANK2 pathogenic variant).• Heterozygotes (carriers) are asymptomatic and are not at risk of developing the disorder (Section "Risk to Family Members, page 13)

#### NBK1233 - MELAS

Question 1 : Quel est le variant pathogène le plus fréquemment identiifé chez les patients atteints de MELAS et dans quel pourcentage de cas est-il retrouvé ?

- The m.3243A>G pathogenic variant in the mitochondrial gene MT-TL1 is present in approximately 80% of individuals with MELAS. (Section "Diagnosis/testing", page 1)

Question 2 : Quelle est la dose journalière de L-carnitine recommandée pour un adulte atteint de MELAS ?

- Adults: 3 g/day in 3 divided doses (Table 6, row "L-carnitine", page 12)

Question 3 : Quelle est la recommandation thérapeutique pour un enfant présentant un épisode aigu de type AVC (stroke-like episode) ?

- Treatment for MELAS is generally supportive. During the acute stroke-like episode, a bolus of intravenous arginine (500 mg/kg for children or 10 g/m2 body surface area for adults) within three hours of symptom onset is recommended followed by the administration of a similar dosage of intravenous arginine as a continuous infusion over 24 hours for the next three to five days (Section "Management", page 1).

Question 4 : Quels examens de surveillance annuels sont recommandés par la fonction rénale et endocrinienne ?

- Renal Urinalysis, Endocrinologic Fasting blood glucose
(Table 7, page 14)


Question 5 : Un homme porteur d'un variant pathogène de l'ADN mitochondrial (ADNmt) responsable du MELAS peut-il transmettre la maladie à ses enfants ?

- The father of a proband is not at risk of having the mtDNA pathogenic variant. (Section "Genetic counseling, page 2)
- The father of a proband does not have the mtDNA pathogenic variant. (Section "Genetic counseling, page 15)

Question 6 : Quel médicament antiépileptique courant doit être formellement évité chez un patient MELAS ?

- (although valproic acid should be avoided). (Section "Agents/circumstances to avoid", pages 2)
- Individuals with MELAS should avoid mitochondrial toxins such as: aminoglycoside antibiotics, linezolid, cigarettes, and alcohol. Valproic acid should be avoided in the treatment of seizures [Lin & Thajeb 2007].Metformin should also be avoided because of its propensity to cause lactic acidosis [Sproule & Kaufmann 2008].Dichloroacetate, which reduces lactate by activating the pyruvate dehydrogenase enzyme, should be avoided in MELAS syndrome. A study evaluating the effect of dichloroacetate in individuals with MELAS syndrome was terminated because of onset or worsening of peripheral neuropathy, indicating that dichloroacetate can be associated with peripheral nerve toxicity [Kaufmann et al 2006a] (Section "Agents/circumstances to avoid", pages 14)
